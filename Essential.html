Essential Elements

First lets talk about the Periodical table to understand more about the Essential Elements!

What is the Periodical Table?

-a table of the chemical elements arranged in order of atomic number, usually in rows, so that elements with similar atomic structure and hence similar chemical properties appear in vertical columns.
The creator of the periodic table, Dmitri Mendeleev, in 1869 began collecting and sorting known properties of elements, like he was playing a game,    while traveling by train. He noticed that there were groups of elements that exhibited similar properties, but he also noticed that there were plenty of exceptions to the emerging patterns.
  
Incredibly, instead of giving up, he tried altering the measured property values to better fit the patterns! He also predicted that certain elements must exist which didn’t at the time – again, in an effort to get the patterns in his "game" to work out.
  
There were plenty of skeptics and it took years to gain international acceptance, but once newly-discovered elements matched the ones that Mendeleev predicted, his patterns could not be dismissed. In addition, some of the properties that he "fudged" were later recalculated and found to be much closer to his predictions.
  

What are Essential Elements?

-Essential elements are macro / micronutrients which are required by living organisms for growth, metabolism and development.

The four most Important Essential Elements:

-These four elements alone make up approximately 96% of all living matter.

    Carbon (C)

    – Regulate the Earth’s temperature, make up the food that sustains us, and provide energy that fuels our global economy.   

    Hydrogen (H)

    – Enable the sun to warm the Earth and help create the water that sustains life.

    Oxygen (0)

    – For us to breathe. Oxygen can help organisms grow, reproduce, and turn food into energy.

    Nitrogen (N)

    – An essential nutrient for the production of amino acids, proteins, nucleic acids, etc.

    What are Compounds Elements?

    Elements are pure substances made up of only one type of atom. Compounds are pure substances made up of two or more different types of elements.

    Example of Compounds Elements

    • Sugar (sucrose - C12H22O11) • Table salt (sodium chloride - NaCl) • Water (H2O) • Carbon dioxide (CO2) • Sodium bicarbonate (baking soda - NaHCO3)
